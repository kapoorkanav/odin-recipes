Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, rich sauces, and savory fillings. Typically, it features alternating layers of marinara or béchamel sauce, seasoned ground meat or vegetables, and a blend of cheeses such as ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. The dish is baked in the oven until the layers meld together, creating a hearty and flavorful casserole with a golden, bubbly top. While the traditional recipe often includes beef or pork, vegetarian and seafood variations are also popular. Lasagna is loved worldwide for its comforting taste and versatility, making it a centerpiece of many family dinners and gatherings.

Ingredients

The ingredients are as follows:

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Italian sausage
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Marinara sauce
  • Onions and garlic
  • Fresh basil and parsley
  • Olive oil

Steps

The steps are as follows The steps are as follows:

  • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  • In a skillet, sauté onions and garlic in olive oil until fragrant, then add sausage and cook until browned.
  • Stir in marinara sauce and simmer for 10 minutes to combine flavors.
  • In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with chopped parsley and a pinch of salt.
  • Layer the lasagna in a baking dish: start with sauce, then noodles, ricotta mixture, and shredded mozzarella and Parmesan.
  • Repeat the layering process until all ingredients are used, ending with a generous layer of cheese on top.
  • Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then uncover and bake for an additional 15 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  • Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
